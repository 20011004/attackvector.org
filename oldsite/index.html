About Me

My name is Hunter John Gregal. I am originally from Swedesboro, New Jersey where I was raised by the best mom I think any one could ask for, Nancy Moore-Gregal. It was here that I developed my fascination with understanding how the world around me worked. I grew sure that I would become a scientist, someone who could use the power of knowledge for good. Following this goal I decided to attended the Gloucester County Institute of Technology high-school where I studied the field of Allied Health in hopes of becoming a scientist. Somewhere during this time I learned 2 major things: One, that I would never pursue a career in the health field. And two, that it was in technology where the power of knowledge would most benefit the world. I began to learn everything that I could on my own time about networking, security, electrical engineering, and even physics while still keeping up with my high-school work. I became the go-to-guy for everyone around me when it came to computer help, phone repairs, and even general "tech" questions. It is this path that has led me to where I am today as a student at Champlain College, where I study Computer Networking and CyberSecurity. In addition to my BS degree, I am pursuing a minor in Digital Forensics in hopes of applying the knowledge of both fields to my future career.
